                <div class="center"><h2><u>General Blood Facts </u></h2></div>
          
                <p>
                <br>
                <br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		There is no substitute for human Blood <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Blood makes up about 7% of your body's weight. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		An average adult has about 14 to 18 pints of Blood. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>       One standard unit or pint of Blood equals about two cups. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Blood carries oxygen and nutrients to all of the body. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Blood carries carbon dioxide and other waste products back to the lungs, kidneys and liver for disposal. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Blood fights against infection and helps heal wounds. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		One unit of donated whole Blood is separated into components before use (red Blood cells, white Blood cells, plasma, platelets, etc.) <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		There are four main Blood types: A, B, AB and O. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Each Blood type is either Rh positive or negative. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		There are about one billion red Blood cells in a few drops of whole Blood. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Red Blood cells live about 120 days in our bodies. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Red Blood cells can be stored under normal conditions for up to 42 days. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Frozen red Blood cells can be stored for ten years, and more. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Platelets must be used within five days. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Platelets are small Blood cells that assist in the process of Blood clotting helping those with leukemia and other cancers, controlling bleeding.<br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Plasma, the fourth major component of Blood, is a sticky, pale yellow fluid mixture of water, protein and salts. It is 95% water. The other 5% is made up of nutrients, proteins and hormones. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Blood Plasma constitutes 55% of the volume of human Blood. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Plasma helps maintain Blood pressure, carries Blood cells, nutrients, enzymes and hormones, and supplies critical proteins for Blood clotting and immunity. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Type AB plasma has been considered as the universal Blood plasma type, and therefore AB plasma is given to patients with any Blood type. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Frozen Plasma can be stored for up to one year. <br><br>
                     <i class="fa-sharp fa-regular fa-circle fa-2xs"></i>		Human Blood; red Blood cells, white Blood cells, plasma and platelets are made naturally by the body in the bone marrow.<br><br>
